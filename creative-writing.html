My Writing Philosophy

My creative writing explores the depth of the human condition through fiction and philosophical reflection. Influenced by authors such as Rainer Maria Rilke, Kahlil Gibran, James Baldwin, Clarice Lispector, David Whyte, and Rumi, I aim to capture emotion, introspection, and layered meaning in every piece. My work blends mystery, moral tension, silence, and symbolism, always anchoring the voice of the voiceless.

I’ve authored works such as The XVII-th: Letters to the One Who Will Come, a reflective novel in letter form and Five Pieces of Sam, a psychological crime thriller. I'm currently working on a new allegorical novel titled The Ivory Vultures, which explores themes of corruption, foreign aid, and the vanishing soul of a nation, set against the backdrop of political deception.

The XVII-th: Letters to the One Who Will Come

The XVII-th is a 166-page philosophical letter-series, written by a vanished voice to the one fated to rise. It is not a guide. It is a mirror. It is a journey through silence, power, betrayal, and the burden of knowing too much, wrapped in metaphor and mystery. Each page seeks to awaken the still, sovereign self buried beneath life’s noise and injustice.

The Ivory Vultures (Coming Soon)

The Ivory Vultures is a political allegory exploring the decay of a fictional nation plagued by corruption, collapsing values, and the manipulation of foreign influence. Told through betrayal, alliances, and raw emotion, it draws readers into the heart of a country teetering on the edge. This upcoming novel builds on the legacy of The XVII-th and aims to spark critical reflection about our collective future.
